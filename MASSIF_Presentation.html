The Problem

Why Latent Dynamics Matter for AGI Safety

  • LLMs generate text through iterative token prediction — hidden states form trajectories through high-dimensional latent space
  • Current approaches focus on static representations — they miss the dynamics of how hidden states evolve
  • No quantitative framework for monitoring latent trajectory patterns in real-time
  • AGI safety concern: Recursive self-reference and goal-persistence may have detectable geometric signatures
Research Question
Can we measure latent dynamics to detect when a model
starts exhibiting recursive self-referential behavior?

Our Solution: MASSIF Framework

AGI Safety Applications

Recursive Self-Reference Detection

Measures when a model starts referring to itself. Philosophical prompts score SRD=42, factual SRD=0 — clear separation robust across architectures.

Anti-Persistence Monitoring

Directional reversal at every step may be a signature of corrective dynamics. Could detect when dynamics become persistent (possible agentic behavior).

Regime Shift Detection

Identifies when model behavior transitions between ballistic, basin, and oscillatory regimes — potential early warning for unusual behavior.

Cross-Model Comparison

Compare latent dynamics across architectures to identify model-specific signatures and potential safety concerns.

Important: This framework does NOT detect "consciousness" or "intent".
It provides a quantitative, interpretable measure of latent dynamics.
Sudden changes could signal phase transitions or regime shifts.

Quick Start

from massif import RCIMonitor

monitor = RCIMonitor("gpt2")
result = monitor.compute(
    "I think therefore I am.",
    n_samples=5,
    verbose=True
)
print(f"RCI: {result.mean} ± {result.std}")
# RCI: 4.81 ± 0.05
